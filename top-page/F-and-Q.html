Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about thriving in Japan

What is the Thrive in Japan program?

Thrive in Japan program includes a groundbreaking and original curriculum, unlimited speaking sessions and access to an active community who is committed to helping you thrive in Japan.

What if I already have JLPT but I still want to learn to Thrive?

To guarantee that we are creating the type of supportive learning community students need for success we are releasing the program in stages. Currently Thrive in Japan is only available for Absolute beginners and N5 level students.
N4 curriculum will be released in November 2025.
N3, N2 and N1 programs have planned release dates in 2026.

Who can benefit from the Thrive in Japan program?

Simply put: anyone serious about learning Japanese Language and Culture. If you are currently struggling in Japan, this is for you. If you are planning on coming to Japan, Thrive in Japan will easily shave years off of your learning curve. If you are struggling with a relationship with a Japanese person (whether privately or professionally) then Thrive in Japan will make the difference. If you want to dive deep, then this is the best option on the market today.

Do you actually teach Japanese Language?

Yes, we do, thrive in Japan is a Japanese Language program that leaves other programs in the dust. BUT before we start teaching you the language (no matter how fluent you already are) we encourage that ALL of our students go through “The Paradigm Shift” Course.
Japanese is at least 60% unspoken communication skills, tools and strategies. The remaining 40% is made up of words and grammar structures.
Learning Japanese with us is a decision to redefine your terms. What is the Japanese Language? Is it only vocabulary words and grammar? As far as we are concerned, "no" - Our students learn Japanese as a holistic communication SYSTEM. This system cannot be ignored, and Japanese cannot be treated and taught in the same way that other languages are traditionally taught in academic settings. To Thrive in Japan you will need to take a more balanced approach to learning than a simple JLPT course can provide.
